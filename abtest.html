Home / A/B Test For Conversion

I’ve runned a lots of successful A/B tests during my career, but I would like to share a big failure.

The context

At UOL we used to have lots of old web pages (because is a 90’s internet company), and things changed really fast.

I was responsible for the website of a group of hosting products and I face a monstrous, unresponsive, ugly, old hiring webpages for web domains.

Well, I’ve done before a lots of conversion tests to non-mobile vs. mobile friendly webpages and even when it was a disaster we have improved the conversion rate at least a little bit.

And it wasn’t just that, the page used to have old information in the menu bar, and the new one above all that have new features, new suggestions, new “go to the top button”.

I mean, c’mon, right? Take a look:

old
new

Well, big disaster: -20%
overall conversion
and -30% GMV

What we’ve learned was that we still have a lot to learn about the process, the mindset of purchases clients.

We’ve jumped to fast to a solution.

Our next step was to look at all funnel data, look at Hotjar for insights, interviewing people and test more prototypes before go live to another test, and the most important thing learned was to have a clear problem to solve not just improve a conversion metric for example.

After that, we were able to find new ways to improve the experience through the funnel.

I think that is a good example because show us that we shouldn’t trust in common sense and know and the first solution that comes to mind usually is not the best, we should look for data, investigate and get deeper in to the customers mind, to seek a real problema to solve.
